# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Mixed professional audience: recruiters scanning for technical depth, clients evaluating capabilities, and industry peers assessing approach and execution quality. Visitors access from desktop (primary) and mobile. They spend 10–30 seconds on the landing area, then navigate to specific projects or contact info. Trust is earned through clarity of output and evidence of systems thinking.

## Product Purpose

Demonstrate technical depth, operations expertise, and systems integration capability. Establish credibility as someone who builds and scales platforms. Surface real projects and work outcomes (not just skills). Enable fast scanning for hiring context while rewarding deeper exploration with well-structured project details. Make contact and collaboration frictionless.

## Brand Personality

**Precise, Efficient, Capable.** Technical no-nonsense. High-competence signal through intentional constraints and clear prioritization. Avoid decoration; every visual choice should serve clarity. Tone is direct, confident, and approachable—not aloof or overcomplicated.

## Anti-references

- Flashy SaaS landing pages (gradient maximalism, hero metrics, hype copy)
- Generic portfolio templates (beige+teal corporate, overly polished, interchangeable)
- Overly cold minimalism (so stark it feels inaccessible or unfriendly)
- Overuse of animation or visual noise that slows scanning

## Design Principles

1. **Signal through constraint.** The brutalist aesthetic itself demonstrates taste: choosing limitation over decoration shows technical judgment.
2. **Prioritize scanning.** Fast navigation, clear hierarchy, project titles and outcomes legible at a glance. Recruiters have limited time.
3. **Let the work speak.** Projects are the hero, not the design. Design supports discovery and trust-building, not self-expression.
4. **Responsive and snappy.** Every interaction should feel instant. No lag, no heavy animations. Efficiency in interaction mirrors efficiency in work.
5. **Systems thinking visible.** Links work predictably, navigation is spatial and anchored, content structure shows care. These details signal someone who builds reliable platforms.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Minimal-tier a11y: prioritize performance and core functionality. Keyboard navigation and focus states present. Color contrast sufficient for readability (acid green on dark background is deliberate and legible). Respect prefers-reduced-motion for animations. No requirement for WCAG AA compliance, but don't intentionally break accessibility.
